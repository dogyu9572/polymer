#Brain Doping Diagnosis (bD²) #Neurochemicals
#LC-MS Analysis
#Animal Model #Clinical Trials
Yo e seph Cho, Seongeun Jeon¹ , Yejin Lee² , Junghyun Son
Doping Control Center, Korea Institute of Science and Technology, Korea
¹Agro-Materials Analysis Team, Korea Agriculture Technology Promotion Agency, Korea
²Research center, KOREA FUNCTIONAL FOOD INSTITUTE, Korea

Brain doping is a novel form of doping that stimulates specific brain regions to enhance sports performance;
however, there are currently no definite provisions or detection methods to address it. This study aimed to establish diagnostic strategies for brain doping, focusing on the fact that it induces alterations in neurochemical concentrations.
First, an advanced method was developed for analyzing 36 neurochemicals in biological samples using liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry, achieving higher sensitivity by employing simple derivatization with ethylchloroformate. The method allowed for rapid detection within 5 minutes and improved the limit of detection compared to conventional methods. An animal model simulating brain doping was established using transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS) in rats, leading to enhanced sports performance and significant changes in urinary neurochemicals. Clinical trials with 15 subjects confirmed that tDCS altered movement-related cortical potentials and improved sports performance.
